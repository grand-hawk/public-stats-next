# Begleitpanzer 57

## Description

The **Begleitpanzer 57** is an Infantry Fighting Vehicle built on the chassis of the German **Marder** IFV, conceived to escort Leopard 2 tanks and engage light armor, helicopters, and infantry with a rapid-fire cannon and anti-tank missiles. The real-world Begleitpanzer 57 was a West German 1980s project that never entered serial production; the design combined a **Bofors 57mm L/70** in a fully stabilized turret with TOW missiles for a flexible fire-support role. Operated by a crew of three — driver, gunner, and commander — on a tracked hull weighing 28.5 tonnes, it is powered by an **MTU MB 833 Ea-500** diesel producing 600 horsepower for a 21.1 hp/t power-to-weight ratio and a top speed of 75 km/h in both directions.\
The main armament is the stabilized **57mm L/70** with a 48-round magazine and a mix of SAP-T, HE-VT, AP-T, and HE-T ammunition, paired with a coaxial **7.62mm MG3** and **BGM-71A TOW** ATGMs. The gunner benefits from a laser rangefinder, fire control system with lead computation, and Gen 1 green thermals at 4x and 12x zoom; the commander has a separate stabilized turret with a CITV featuring 2x and 8x magnification and laser rangefinder for target handoff.
