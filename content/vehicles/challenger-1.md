# Challenger 1

## Description

The **Challenger 1** is a Main Battle Tank that entered British Army service in 1983, replacing the Chieftain and seeing combat in the Gulf War. It was built by Royal Ordnance (later Vickers) and combined the Chieftain's **120mm L11A5** gun with a new hull, improved armour, and a **Perkins CV12** diesel. The 62.5 t tracked vehicle is crewed by four — driver, gunner, commander, and loader — and reaches 56 km/h forward and 38 km/h in reverse. The CV12 develops 1,314 hp for a 21 hp/t power-to-weight ratio and neutral steering.\
Main armament is the stabilized **120mm L11A5** with laser rangefinder and FCS (10x day, lead); secondary weapons are the coaxial **7.62mm L8A2** and commander **7.62mm L37A2**. Smoke grenades and an engine smoke system provide defensive screening.
This vehicle features a single addon: **Mk3 Upgrade**. The Mk3 Upgrade costs 1250 points and equips the Challenger 1 with ROMOR-A ERA to the hull sides and LFP; significantly increasing protection against CE munitions. Additionally, the upgrade provides a Gen 2 thermal sight, which grants superior target identification and situational awareness over the stock optics.
