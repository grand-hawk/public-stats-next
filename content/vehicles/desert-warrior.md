# Desert Warrior

## Description

The **Desert Warrior** is an Infantry Fighting Vehicle, a British export variant of the FV510 Warrior fitted with an American turret carrying the **25mm M242 Bushmaster** and **BGM-71C I-TOW** missiles, developed for Kuwait and other Gulf customers. It weighs 29 tonnes with a crew of three and seven passengers, and is tracked. The **Perkins CV-8** diesel produces 550 hp for 19 hp/t and a top speed of 75 km/h in both directions, with neutral steering.\
The turret has a stabilizer, smoke grenades, **7.62mm M240** coaxial, and a fire control system with laser rangefinder, lead, and Gen 2 green thermals (3.3x–10x). The commander has a stabilized sight with laser and 2x–8x zoom.
This vehicle features two addons: **Improved Autocannon** and **Improved Missiles**. The Improved Autocannon add-on costs 250 points and replaces the Desert Warrior’s stock M791 APDS with M919 DU APFSDS; this significantly increases the Bushmaster's effectiveness against armor by providing a 21 mm increase in maximum penetration (from 81 mm to 102 mm) and a 2x spall multiplier. The Improved Missiles add-on costs 500 points and replaces the stock BGM-71C I-TOW with the BGM-71F TOW 2 series. This upgrade introduces two distinct missile variants: the TOW 2A, which features a tandem warhead and an additional 200 mm of penetration (from 800 mm to 1000 mm), and the TOW 2B, which utilizes a "top-attack" flight profile to strike the thinner roof armor of enemy vehicles with 150 mm of penetration.
