# Merkava Mk.1B

## Description

The **Merkava Mk.1B** is a Main Battle Tank and the first production model of the Israeli **Merkava** family, designed in the 1970s with the engine forward for crew protection and a low, angled hull; it entered service in 1979 and saw action in Lebanon. The vehicle is tracked, weighs 61 tonnes, and is crewed by four — driver, gunner, commander, and loader — and is powered by an **AVDS-1790-6A** diesel engine producing 908 horsepower for a 14.9 hp/t power-to-weight ratio and a top speed of 54 km/h forward and 11 km/h in reverse, with neutral steering.\
The main armament is the **105mm Sharir** (license-built L7-type) with a 6.7-second reload and M156, M111, M416, and M152 ammunition, plus a coaxial **7.62mm FN MAG 60-40**; the commander has a roof **60mm Soltam** mortar and a second MAG. The gun has a two-plane stabilizer and a laser warning system; the gunner uses a laser rangefinder, fire control system, and Gen 1 green thermals at 8×, and the commander has a 4×–20× periscope with laser. An engine smoke system is fitted.
This vehicle features four add-ons: **.50 Coaxial**, **M413**, **Smoke Grenades** and **Chains**. The .50 Coaxial add-on costs 250 points and equips the gunner with an additional 12.7mm M2 machine gun, significantly improving the tank's effectiveness against infantry and obstructing foliage. The M413 add-on costs 500 points and replaces the stock M111 rounds with M413 APFSDS, providing a substantial 74mm increase in maximum penetration (from 334mm to 408mm) for enhanced anti-armor performance. The Smoke Grenades add-on costs 150 points and installs 12 smoke grenade launchers on the turret to facilitate rapid obscurement during tactical maneuvers. Finally, the Chains add-on costs 50 points and equips the rear of the turret with protective chain mail, offering specialized, albeit minimal, protection against incoming HEAT warheads.
