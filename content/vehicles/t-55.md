# T-55

## Description

The **T-55** is a Main Battle Tank and the successor to the T-54, entering Soviet service in 1958 and becoming one of the most produced tanks in history with tens of thousands built and exported. Designed at the Kharkiv Morozov design bureau, it introduced a nuclear-biological-chemical protection system, improved engine, and a redesigned turret while retaining the **100mm D-10** family of guns. The 36-tonne tracked vehicle is crewed by four and powered by a **ChTZ V-55V** diesel rated at 580 horsepower, giving 16.1 hp/t and a top speed of 50 km/h.\
Main armament is the stabilized **100mm D-10TS2** with 3.5x and 7x magnification (no laser rangefinder); secondary weapons are a coaxial **7.62mm PKT** and a loader-operated **12.7mm DSHK**. The T-55 has an engine smoke system and no FCS or APS, relying on manual rangefinding and stabilizer-assisted firing on the move.
