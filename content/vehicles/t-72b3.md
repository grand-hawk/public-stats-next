# T-72B3

## Description

The **T-72B3** is a Main Battle Tank and a Russian Army modernization of the T-72B, introduced in the 2010s to extend the service life of existing hulls with a new engine, sighting, and improved ERA. The T-72 has been the backbone of Soviet and Russian armored forces since the 1970s; the B3 upgrade brings the platform closer to contemporary fire-control and mobility standards without a full replacement. A crew of three operates the 46-tonne tracked vehicle, powered by a **ChTZ V-84MS** diesel (840 hp, 18.3 hp/t) for a top speed of just over 61 km/h.\
Main armament is a stabilized **125mm 2A46M-5** with autoloader, firing 3BM46 APFSDS, **9M119** Refleks ATGM, 3BK18M HEAT, and 3OF26 HE; the gunner has a laser rangefinder, FCS with lead and tracking, Gen 2 thermals, and 4x/12x zoom. A coaxial **7.62mm PKT** and a commander's **12.7mm KORD** round out the armament; smoke grenades and engine smoke are fitted.
This vehicle features two add-ons: **T-72B3M** and **Turtle Addon**. The T-72B3M upgrade costs 500 points and provides a comprehensive modernisation by equipping the gunner with high-resolution Gen 3 thermal sights, adding ERA bags to the hull sides for improved lateral protection, and upgrading the primary missile to the 9M119M1 Tandem ATGM, which boasts a 150 mm increase in maximum penetration (jumping from 700 mm to 850 mm) for superior long-range lethality. The Turtle Addon is free and converts the tank into the previously Fish State "Tutel" configuration by installing a massive protective "Barn" designed to defeat RPGs, drones, and ATGMs, though this immense physical protection comes at the direct cost of severely restricted visibility, reduced mobility, and a significantly larger, more vulnerable silhouette. The T-72B3M and Turtle add-ons cannot be used together.
