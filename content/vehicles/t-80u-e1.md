# T-80U-E1

## Description

The **T-80U-E1** is a Main Battle Tank representing an upgraded Russian **T-80U** with an improved stabilizer, better thermals, and a retrofitted autoloader for faster reloads. The T-80U entered service in the 1980s as the first Soviet production tank with a gas turbine engine. Weighing 46 tonnes with a three-man crew, it is powered by a **GTD-1250** turbine producing 1,250 horsepower (27.2 hp/t) and reaches 70 km/h forward and 11.1 km/h in reverse — notably better reverse than the typical T-72/90 family. Main armament is the **125mm 2A46M-1** with a 6-second autoloader cycle, firing **3BM59** APFSDS, **3BK18M** HEAT, **9M119M1** ATGMs, and HE-Frag; a coaxial **7.62mm PKT** and commander’s **12.7mm NSVT** are fitted. The vehicle has a stabilizer, smoke grenades, and engine smoke. The gunner has a laser rangefinder, fire control with lead and tracking, and second-generation white-hot thermals.
